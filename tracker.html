Oct 2023 🇺🇸 US
BIS · Dept of Commerce
 Export Controls Expanded H100/H200 GPU export restrictions to China and additional countries; closed performance density loopholes in Oct 2022 rules. Tightens the Oct 2022 framework significantly. Key implication: Chinese cloud providers now face meaningful compute ceilings, accelerating Huawei Ascend adoption domestically.
Mar 2024 🇺🇸 US
Dept of State
 Diplomacy Pax Silica initiative announced — US diplomatic push to build semiconductor supply chain alliances with allied nations. Signals a shift toward technology diplomacy as foreign policy. Watch for bilateral MoUs with India, Japan, Netherlands as follow-through indicators.
Feb 2024 🇮🇳 India
MeitY · Cabinet
 Industrial Policy India approves three semiconductor projects under ISM: Tata Electronics (fab), CG Power (OSAT), Kaynes Semicon (OSAT) — total investment ~$15B. Historic milestone for India's fab ambitions. Execution risk remains high; OSAT approvals are more credible near-term than the Tata fab timeline.
Sep 2023 🇨🇳 China
NDRC · MIIT
 Industrial Policy China's "Big Fund III" ($47.5B state fund) launched targeting advanced chip design, equipment, and materials to counter US export controls. Largest single mobilisation of state capital in semiconductor history. Focus on equipment and materials suggests a strategic pivot away from pure fab scaling toward supply chain self-sufficiency.
Sep 2023 🇪🇺 EU
European Commission
 Industrial Policy EU Chips Act officially enters into force, targeting 20% of global chip production by 2030 with €43B in public and private investment. Ambitious target faces headwinds: European chip demand is largely automotive/industrial, not leading-edge. TSMC Dresden fab is the centrepiece bet — success depends on sustained subsidy and demand certainty.
Jan 2025 🇺🇸 US
BIS · Dept of Commerce
 Export Controls Biden administration's "AI diffusion rule" — tiered framework restricting advanced AI chip exports globally, with allies on Tier 1 and most countries on Tier 2/3. Most sweeping extraterritorial tech control in decades. Creates friction with Middle East and Southeast Asia partners who want AI compute access without full alignment with US security requirements.
